‚ö° Key Concepts to Remember

Essential ideas that make LLMs work

üéØ Next Word Prediction

At its core, an LLM is trying to predict the most likely next word in a sequence. This simple task, done incredibly well, creates the illusion of understanding and reasoning.

üìä Statistical Patterns

LLMs don't "know" facts like humans do. Instead, they've learned statistical relationships between words and concepts from massive amounts of text data.

üîç Attention Mechanism

The breakthrough that made modern LLMs possible. Attention lets the model focus on relevant parts of the input, just like how you focus on specific words when reading.

üé≠ Emergent Abilities

As models get larger, they develop capabilities that weren't explicitly programmed - like reasoning, creativity, and problem-solving. These "emerge" from scale.

üîÑ Training vs. Inference

Training is like studying for years to become an expert. Inference is like applying that expertise to answer questions in real-time conversations.

‚öñÔ∏è No True Understanding

LLMs are incredibly sophisticated pattern matchers, but they don't "understand" the world like humans do. They excel at predicting plausible responses, not truth.
